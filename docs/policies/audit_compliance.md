# Audit Compliance & Best Practices Guide

**Target Audience**: Firm Owners & Administrators  
**Goal**: Ensuring your digital ledger matches your physical financial state.

## 1. The Reconciliation Cycle
To maintain a professional and audit-ready ledger, we recommend a **Daily Reconciliation Workflow**:

1.  **Record Collections**: As payments are received (Cash/UPI/Bank), record them immediately in the **Payments** module.
2.  **Cashbook Audit**: At the end of the business day, use the **Cashbook** to enter your physical denominations.
3.  **Cross-Check**: Ensure the "Total Cash Collected" in your Payments report for that day matches the total in your Cashbook.

## 2. Payout Settlements
When an auction winner is paid:
- **Record the Payout**: Use the "Record Payout" action in the **Auctions** or **Settlements** module.
- **Voucher Generation**: Always print or save the **Payout Voucher** (PDF/Print) generated by the system.
- **Physical Signature**: We strongly recommend keeping a physical folder for signed payout vouchers as a secondary audit trail.

## 3. Dealing with Market Debt
Market Debt (Total Paid-Out Liabilities) represents your firm's most critical financial risk.
- Use the **Analytics Dashboard** to monitor the ratio of Market Debt to Total Collections.
- Keep the **Defaulters Report** updated. A "Defaulter" in ChitVault is a member whose digital dues have aged past the grace period.

## 4. Addressing Errors
If a mathematical error is detected:
- **Correction Logs**: Use the "Void/Modify" actions sparingly. The system logs these activities for auditing.
- **Notes**: Always use the `Notes` field when recording a payment or auction to explain any manual overrides or special circumstances.

## 5. Staff Accountability
- Only provide Staff roles to employees who handle data entry.
- Regularly review the **Activity Logs** in the Admin panel to see who recorded which payments.
- Periodically change PIN locks for added local security.
